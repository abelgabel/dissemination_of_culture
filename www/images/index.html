Tribute model

ABM

Question

How new political actors can emerge from an aggregation of small political actors?

Example of global actors: European Union, NAFTA, Soviet Union, United States, Italy, etc.

Over the centuries global actors have emerged and disintegrate as the result of two mechanisms coersion and extorsion. In 1956, Hungary announced to withdraw from the Warsaw Pact but the Soviet Union occupied and install a new Soviet government. The states of Arizona, California, New Mexico, Nevada, Utah were obtained after the Mexican-America war and a symbolic payment to the mexican government.

In 1997, Robert Axelrod propose the tribute model. In his model, actors may demand tributes to other actors, they may refuse to pay a tribute and instead go into war. As a result of tributes and wars, actors form alliances. Alliances protect poor actors, they cause world wars, they have a tremendous impact on global wealth; but alliances are not exempt from civil wars.

The tribute model consists of

  1. 10 actors arrangend in a cycle.
  2. There is only one resource: wealth.

Period 1:

  1. Each agent is given an initial wealth drawn from a uniform distribution([300,500]).
  2. There is no commitment between the actors.
  3. Three actors become active, which means they can demand a tribute of 250 from a neighbor, the target actor.
  4. The target actor decides to pay the tribute or go into war.
  5. If there is a war both actors lose 25% of the wealth of the other actor.
  6. If there is no war, then the commitment between the two actors increased by 10%

Subsequent periods:

  1. The wealth of all actors is increased by 250.
  2. Each period three actors are chosen at random and become active (A1, A2, A3). An active actor Ai choose a target actor, Bi, and it demands a tribute of 250.
  3. The target actor can be a neighbour or an actor Bi such that all actors between Ai and Bi have a stronger commitment to Ai than to Bi.
  4. If actor Bi decides not to fight, then the commitment between the two actors increases by 10% (up to 100%)
  5. If there is war between them :
    1. An actor C choose to support actor Ai, if
    2. C has a stronger support to actor Ai than to actor Bi, and
    3. All actors between Ai and C or Bi and C support actor Ai.
    4. Similarly, it may choose to support actor Bi.
    5. If C has equal commitment, it remains neutral.
    If there is war:
    1. A commited actor joins forces (wealth) proportional to its commitment.
    2. A commited actor lose 25% of the total wealth of the other alliance times its proportion of wealth commited to the cause.
    3. The commitments between all actors in the same alliance are increased by 10% (up to 100%); and the commitments across actors in different alliances decrease by 10% (commitment is never lower than 0%).
